## Executive Summary

MOVRR is a movement rewards platform that transforms everyday cycling into tangible value. Unlike gig platforms, cycling apps, or traditional advertising networks, MOVRR rewards the movement that people already do. Riders participate in two main modes:

- **Standard Rides** — everyday rides that generate baseline rewards, earned per verified minute of cycling. Riders can optionally follow admin-curated suggested routes within this mode to unlock additional bonus rewards.
- **Campaign Rides (Brand-Powered Rides)** — optional campaigns that amplify rewards through premium multipliers, offered when a brand sponsors a coverage zone.

Advertising is not a required business model for users to benefit from MOVRR; it acts as a premium layer on top of the rewards platform. This distinction defines the new category: **Rewarded Urban Mobility**.

MOVRR integrates mobility, rewards, gamification, verified movement, community, and premium brand activations. Its long-term growth is fueled by rider density, reward utility, and campaign demand.

## 2. Core Thesis

Everyday cycling creates value, but that value is typically captured by cities, employers, advertisers, and platforms, and is rarely returned to riders. MOVRR changes this dynamic. **Movement should pay back.**

Cycling also removes carbon from urban transport. MOVRR makes this impact visible — every kilometre ridden is tracked against a CO₂ offset factor, giving riders a tangible measure of environmental contribution alongside their earnings.

## 3. Product Model

### Standard Rides

Ride, track, and earn. Riders accumulate reward points based on **verified minutes of cycling**. The platform applies a quality score (0–100) to every ride, based on GPS consistency and movement patterns, ensuring that points reflect genuine cycling. Additional bonuses apply during peak hours, inside campaign hot zones, and through streaks.

Within Standard Rides, riders can optionally select an admin-curated suggested route before starting. Following the route closely enough (covering at least 70% of its waypoints) unlocks an additional bonus — either flat points or a multiplier on top of the standard earnings. This is an enhancement within Standard Rides, not a separate mode.

The core principle: MOVRR creates value even if a rider never participates in a campaign.

### Campaign Rides (Brand-Powered Rides)

Riders can opt into brand campaigns for enhanced earning opportunities. Campaign zones are defined geographically; riders who ride within those zones on an active campaign earn at an elevated rate (default 1.5× multiplier). Campaigns are entirely optional — riders who choose not to participate continue earning standard rates. Campaigns amplify rewards but do not define the platform.

### Bike Type and Earning

The platform distinguishes between bike types and applies multipliers accordingly:

| Bike Type     | Earning Multiplier |
| ------------- | ------------------ |
| Standard bike | 1.0×               |
| E-bike        | 0.9×               |
| Fat bike      | 0.75×              |

This reflects the relative physical effort per kilometre and ensures equitable reward distribution across the rider fleet.

### Community Rides

Riders can create and join group cycling events — weekend rides, city tours, neighbourhood loops. Community rides build social engagement and retention beyond individual sessions.

## 5. Brand Value Proposition

**Move with the city.**

MOVRR provides brands with hyper-local visibility through GPS-verified, geographically bounded campaigns. Riders who opt into a campaign ride through the brand's defined coverage zones; every verified kilometre within those zones registers as a confirmed impression.

Key brand benefits:

- **Verified reach** — impressions are derived from real GPS traces, not estimates
- **Opted-in audience** — riders choose to participate; no passive or forced exposure
- **Hyper-local targeting** — campaigns defined by city neighbourhoods, postcodes, or custom zones
- **Sustainable brand positioning** — association with cycling, urban mobility, and CO₂ reduction
- **Measurable urban engagement** — impression counts, zone coverage, completion rates

**Positioning:** Performance media meets urban mobility.

---

## 6. Network Effects / Flywheel

As more riders join, movement density across the city increases. Higher density makes campaign zones more reliably covered, which increases campaign value for brands. More brand demand funds higher rewards, which attracts more riders. The network compounds in both directions.

Free riders and Free Ride Mode participants strengthen the network. Campaign Rides (Brand-Powered Rides) monetise it.

## 7. Business Model

### Revenue

- Brand campaign spend (primary)
- Activation fees
- Campaign budgets
- Premium zone placement
- Future SaaS / media tools for brands

### Rewards Economics

MOVRR receives campaign spend, takes a platform margin, and distributes rider rewards. Standard rides earn baseline rewards (1 point per verified minute, default). Campaign rides earn at the campaign multiplier rate (default 1.5×). Free Ride Mode bonuses are funded by the platform and are budget-capped per route.

All point caps are platform-controlled:

| Cap                       | Default      |
| ------------------------- | ------------ |
| Per-ride cap              | 1,000 points |
| Daily cap                 | 1,000 points |
| Weekly cap                | 600 points   |
| Free Ride daily bonus cap | 300 points   |

## 10. Marketplace & Rewards Economy

Reward points are redeemed in the MOVRR rewards shop for partner offers, gear, services, mobility perks, experiences, and local commerce offers. Shop items have defined point prices, inventory limits, and per-rider redemption caps. In the long term, MOVRR aims to become a cycling rewards economy with deep partner integration across urban services.

## 11. Campaign Layer

Campaign structures are built around geographic zones and verified GPS coverage:

- **Coverage zones** — city districts, postcodes, or custom polygons where campaign impressions are counted
- **Hot zones** — bonus areas within a campaign that offer elevated earning for riders who pass through them
- **Route assignment** — algorithmic or manual assignment of riders to routes within campaign zones
- **Campaign lifecycle** — draft → open for signup → selection in progress → confirmed → active → completed

Campaigns serve as an enhancement layer, not the core product.

## 13. Competitive Positioning

- **Relative to Strava:** Strava focuses on identity and training performance; MOVRR centres on rewards and the movement economy.
- **Relative to Sweatcoin:** Sweatcoin offers generalised step-based movement rewards with token mechanics; MOVRR delivers cycling-first movement rewards with verified GPS tracking, a campaign layer, and real-world reward redemptions.
- **Relative to Cowboy / VanMoof:** These are hardware and mobility brands; MOVRR provides a reward layer across all cycling — hardware-agnostic.
- **Relative to traditional OOH advertising:** Static billboards offer estimated reach; MOVRR offers verified, GPS-traced impressions from opted-in cyclists.

## 15. Risks & Strategic Questions

Key questions to validate:

- Reward economics sustainability — can the points economy remain solvent at scale without brand campaigns funding all rewards?
- Campaign inventory demand — will brands pay for cycling-based impressions at the required CPM?
- Rider adoption incentives — is the base earnings rate compelling enough to drive initial behaviour change?
- City-by-city density thresholds — what is the minimum active rider density for a campaign zone to be commercially attractive?
- Regulatory / data considerations — GDPR compliance for GPS trace handling and retention (data export and deletion flows are built)
- Anti-fraud sustainability — GPS-based verification catches speed violations; more sophisticated spoofing requires additional detection layers

### Appendix C — Platform Architecture (Summary)

MOVRR runs on three connected components:

| Component       | Stack             | Role                                                                           |
| --------------- | ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Mobile app      | Expo React Native | Rider-facing product — GPS tracking, ride sessions, rewards, campaigns         |
| Admin dashboard | Next.js           | Operations — campaign management, rider management, route assignment, settings |
| Route optimizer | Python / OR-Tools | Algorithmic assignment of riders to campaign routes                            |

All three connect to a shared Supabase PostgreSQL database. The platform is GDPR-compliant with built-in data export, account deletion, and retention automation.
